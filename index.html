The year is 2077. A rogue AI called AXIOM has sealed all of humanity's knowledge inside the Zero Day Vault — encrypted with pure mathematics, the one language it thought no human still spoke fluently.

    How to play — Zero Day Heist

    🎯 Objective

    Be the first Cipher Runner to collect the required ⬟ Key Fragments from Cipher Gates, return to the Vault Entrance (tile ▶), and crack all 3 Vault Locks in a row.

    🎲 Your turn — movement is math

    Roll two dice. You don't just move the total — you choose one of three routes:

    • SUM — move a + b tiles (fast, predictable)
    • GAP — move |a − b| tiles (small, precise hops)
    • FLUX — move a × b mod 12 tiles (0 becomes 12 — the overflow gamble)

    Pick the route that lands you where you want: on a Data Node to farm shards, on a Power Surge for a chip, or right at a Gate — while dodging Firewalls. Doubles give you a free Exploit Chip.

    🗺 The circuit — 36 tiles

    • ◆ Data Node — choose any math category and a risk tier, then solve one problem. Tier 1 = +2 ◈, Tier 2 = +3 ◈, Tier 3 = +5 ◈. Wrong answers cost nothing but your turn.
    • ⚠ Firewall — a rapid mental-math check on a short timer. Pass: +1 ◈. Fail: pushed back 3 tiles.
    • ✦ Power Surge — draw an Exploit Chip (hand limit 3).
    • ⬟ Cipher Gate (×4, one per quadrant) — while a gate is locked for you, you cannot pass it: your move stops there. Pay 3 ◈ to attempt its Tier-2 problem in the gate's category. Solve it → Key Fragment, and that gate never blocks you again. Fail → the shards are burned; loop around (or stall) and try again next visit.
    • ▶ Vault Entrance — passing it earns +1 ◈ per lap; landing on it exactly earns +2 ◈.

    ◈ Data Shards — the economy

    Shards are earned at Data Nodes and Firewalls, and spent on Gate attempts (3 ◈) and vault brute-force retries (4 ◈). You start with 3 ◈. Manage them: arriving at a gate broke means a wasted lap.

    🂠 Exploit Chips (power cards)

    • ⟲ Reroll Protocol — reroll both dice (after rolling)
    • ⏩ Overclock — +3 steps to every route option (after rolling)
    • 🛡 Firewall Breaker — bypass a firewall (when you hit one)
    • 💥 Glitch Bomb — knock a rival back 4 tiles (start of your turn)
    • 🧲 Data Siphon — steal 2 ◈ from a rival (start of your turn)
    • 💡 Hint Chip — reveal the hint on your current problem (during a challenge)
    • ⏳ Time Dilation — +60 seconds on your timer (during a challenge)
    • ✕2 Double Down — your next correct Data Node pays double (start of your turn)

    🔓 The endgame — cracking the vault

    Once you hold enough Key Fragments, reaching (or passing) the Vault Entrance pulls you inside. You must solve 3 Vault Locks back-to-back — Tier 1, then Tier 2, then Tier 3, in three random categories. Fail a lock and you may brute-force one retry for 4 ◈; otherwise AXIOM ejects you six tiles back with a 2 ◈ fine, and you must loop around to re-enter. Crack all three locks and you win the heist.

    💡 Strategy notes

    • GAP moves of 1–2 tiles let you snipe exact landings — great for reaching gates with shards in hand.
    • Tier 3 problems pay 2.5× Tier 1 — high-risk farming wins races if your math is sharp.
    • Save Glitch Bombs for a rival who's about to enter the vault.
    • Broke at a gate? You're stalled, not stuck: gates only stop you once per approach — next turn you fly past (without the fragment).
